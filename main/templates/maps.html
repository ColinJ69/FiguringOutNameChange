Predicted spread of PFAS across NC water systems

Info about map



These 130 locations across North Carolina effect a total population of about 6 million people, of which machine learning(ML) models have predicted that 4 million of those people are exposed to 1-6 of the tested PFAS and/or GenX chemicals at dangerous thresholds.





How the ML model was trained


I first selected a training and testing set from the NC PFAS Network, then using 12 features such as distance from potential industrial polluters, users of AFFF firefighting foam, and the depth of the well(if the source is a well). Using a 75/25 train-test split, the most accurate model was the light gradient boosting machine at 94.87% accuracy.
